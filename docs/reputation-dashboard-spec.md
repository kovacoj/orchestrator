# Signal Foundry — Reputation Monitor Dashboard Spec

Source of truth for the Prague coffee-chain reputation-monitor demo dashboard
generated by the orchestrator under `frontend/`.

This spec is verbatim from the project brief. Implementation lives in
`frontend/` and uses local artificial data only (no live backend in the first
version).

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Build a demo-ready B2B SaaS analytics dashboard for **Signal Foundry**, focused
on a Prague coffee-shop chain with 10 locations. The dashboard simulates how a
business operator monitors internal operating data and external public signals
to detect business risks early.

Demo story:

> A Miners Coffee-like Prague coffee chain operates 10 locations. The system
> monitors daily revenue, hourly transactions, staff coverage, menu prices,
> customer reviews, complaint topics, competitor prices, and public reputation
> signals. It detects that the **Vinohrady** location has suffered a
> **23% sentiment drop**. The likely cause is **slow service during the
> 8–9 AM morning peak**, supported by higher queue pressure and below-normal
> staffing. A nearby competitor promotion is also detected but is treated as
> secondary context rather than primary cause. The dashboard recommends adding
> **one morning-shift staff member for three days** and monitoring sentiment
> recovery.

The dashboard must be explanatory. Every chart, alert, and prediction must
explain **what was found**, **why it matters**, **what evidence supports it**,
and **what to do next**.

## 6. Main incident

- Location: Vinohrady, LOC_001
- Incident window: most recent 7–10 days
- Baseline sentiment ≈ 0.70 → recent ≈ 0.54 (drop ≈ 23%)
- Topic spike: slow service and queue complaints
- Queue pressure spike: 8–9 AM and 9–10 AM
- Staffing: one fewer than normal on several affected mornings
- External context: Competitor A morning promotion 10–15% off cappuccino/flat white

## 13. Tone & safety rules

- Use phrasing like "Evidence suggests…", "Likely cause is…",
  "Treated as secondary context…", "Confidence: 0.89".
- NEVER attribute issues to individual employees.
- NEVER claim guaranteed recovery.
- ALWAYS show a visible "Artificial demo data" disclaimer.

## 14. Acceptance

A hackathon judge can:

1. Open dashboard and see the product story.
2. See top alert: "Vinohrady sentiment dropped 23%".
3. Understand slow-service morning peak as likely cause.
4. See evidence from sentiment, complaints, queue, staffing.
5. See competitor promotion as **secondary** context.
6. See concrete recommendation: add one morning staff member for 3 days.
7. Open Predictions and compare no-action vs extra-staff scenarios.
8. Open Research Labs and see selected vs suppressed signals.
9. Open Reports and read a clear business summary.
10. See visible disclaimer that all data is artificial.

## 15. Non-negotiable

1. Demo-ready visually.
2. Main alert: Vinohrady -23% sentiment.
3. Recommended action: one morning staff member for 3 days.
4. Competitor promo = secondary, not main cause.
5. Staff attribution suppressed.
6. Every chart has interpretation.
7. Every prediction has uncertainty.
8. Every finding has evidence.
9. Artificial-data disclaimer visible.
10. No live API/DB needed.
